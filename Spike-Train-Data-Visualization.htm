Spike Train Data Visualization - Rastor Plots, Peri-Stimulus Time Histograms and Heat Maps:¶

This example uses many methods to display spike train data. The data comes from an experiment where subjects were shown a green light for a period of time (2000-3000ms), and spike train measurements were taken from multiple neurons. The data in this example covers only the m1_6 labeled neuron in order to avoid being too similar to assignments from NESC 3505. Rastor plots are used to show the timing of spikes for each repetition for the control condition at a contrast of 4. The peri-stimulus time histogram shows the frequency of spikes for 50ms time intervals also for the control condition at a contrast of 4. These two plots are shown as complimentary subplots below. Heat maps show the frequency of spikes over time and contrasts for the control and adaptation conditions.

df = pd.read_csv('crowder_3_neurons.csv')

#Creating a dataframe of data from just one neuron:
df_1 = df[df['neuron'] == 'm1_6']

Rastor and Peri-Stimulus Time Histograms:¶

The rastor and peri-stimulus time histograms display the spike time data of the first repetition at a contrast of 4. The period of time where the green light was presented is also displayed on the two plots. This gives a good visualization of when the neurons are most likely to spike in relation to the stimuli.

#Creating the figure in which to display the two subplots
fig = plt.figure(figsize=[10,5])

ax = plt.subplot(2, 1, 1)

#Creating a list to operate the for loop:
repetitions = [1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6]

#Creating a rastor plot across the repetitions:
for rep in repetitions: 
    spike_times = [i for i, x in enumerate(df_1[(df_1['repetition']==rep) & (df_1['contrast']==4) & (df_1['condition']=='CTRL')]['spike']) if x==1]
    plt.vlines(spike_times, rep-1, rep) 
    plt.axvspan(2000, 3000, alpha=0.1, color='g') 
    plt.ylim([0, 6])
plt.title('Rastor Plot and Peri-Stimulus Time Histogram for Each Control Repitition at Contrast 4')
plt.ylabel('Repetition')
plt.yticks([x + 0.5 for x in range(6)], [str(x + 1) for x in range(6)])
plt.xlim([-1, 4000])

#Assigning spike times for all repetitions to a value:
all_spike_times = df_1[(df_1['condition']=='CTRL') & (df_1['contrast']==4) & (df_1['spike']==1)]['time']

#Plotting the peri-stimulus time histogram for all repetitions
ax = plt.subplot(2, 1, 2)

plt.axvspan(2000, 3000, alpha=0.25, color='g')
spike_count, bins = np.histogram(all_spike_times, bins=80)
plt.bar(bins[:-1], spike_count, width=50)

plt.xlabel('Time (ms)')
plt.ylabel('Number of Spikes that Occured')
plt.xlim([-1, 4000])
plt.tight_layout()
plt.show()

Heat Maps:¶

To begin, I created labels for the conditions and contrasts and made a dataframe of their values. I then created a histogram of the spike_time values and appended it to an empty list. The label dataframe is then concatenated with the spike_times histogram, which is converted into a dataframe. The index is then set to the labels specified.

#Creating values to loop through:
conditions = (df_1['condition'].unique())
contrasts = sorted(df_1['contrast'].unique())

#Creating a dataframe of labels:
condition_labels = pd.DataFrame([[condition, contrast] for condition in conditions for contrast in contrasts], columns=['condition', 'contrast'])

#Creating a list of spike times from a histogram:
psth_df_list = []

for condition in conditions:
    for contrast in contrasts:
        spike_times = df[(df['condition']==condition) & (df['contrast']==contrast) & (df['spike']==1)]['time']
        hist, bins = np.histogram(spike_times,
                          bins=80)
        psth_df_list.append(hist)

#Concatenating the list and condition labels:
psth_df = pd.concat([condition_labels, pd.DataFrame(psth_df_list)], axis=1)

#Setting the index to the condition labels:
psth_df = psth_df.set_index(['condition','contrast'])

Finally, I plotted the heat maps. In order to get the x axis to display proper times, I had to specify time labels and bin widths.

#Creating bins for the heat map:
hist_bin_width = 50 
time_labels = list(np.arange(4000))
time_bins = np.arange(0, max(time_labels), hist_bin_width)

#Plotting the heatmaps of each condition:
fig = plt.figure(figsize=[15,30])
subplot_counter = 1 # used to track subplots

for condition in conditions:

    ax = fig.add_subplot(2, 2, subplot_counter)
    aa = plt.imshow(psth_df.loc[condition], origin='lower', cmap='hot', interpolation='bicubic', aspect=5)

    xticks = range(0, len(time_bins), 10)
    plt.xticks(xticks, time_bins[xticks])

    plt.yticks([x for x, y in enumerate(contrasts)], 
               [x for x in contrasts])
    if condition == conditions[0]:
        plt.ylabel('Stimulus Contrast')

    plt.title(condition)
    plt.xlabel('Time (ms)')

    plt.tight_layout()
    subplot_counter += 1

    cb = fig.colorbar(aa, shrink=0.20)

plt.show()
